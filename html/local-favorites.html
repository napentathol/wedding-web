Breakfast/Brunch

Alex and I love breakfast foods and Bloomington has some good spots for the (best?) meal of the day. Check out The Village Deli for all-day breakfast options including giant pancakes and Paxton’s Potatoes (a Village Deli speciality that Courtney always orders). Runcible Spoon is another good all-day breakfast spot AND they serve pitchers of mimosas. If you want to treat yo’ self head to The Uptown Cafe for your first meal of the day—breakfast is served until early afternoon and they’re pretty popular for brunch for look into making a reservation if you’re thinking of eating here

For the Adventurous

For a small town in Indiana, Bloomington boasts an incredible selection of international cuisine. Most of these restaurants are located downtown on 4th Street. There you will find Thai (Siam House), Tibetan (Little Tibet), Turkish (Anatolia), Korean (Do), Cajun/Creole (Dats), and Indian (Taste of India) food options. Not located on 4th Street but still in downtown Bloomington you can find Greek food (The Trojan Horse), Afghani cuisine (Samira), and classic Irish Pub food (The Irish Lion)

Bloomington Classics

If you’re going out with a group or want a nicer option for lunch or dinner FARMBloomington and Malibu Grill are both excellent options that serve crowd-pleasers. For dinner at either place a reservation would probably be a good idea. If you want to go more casual the hot wings at BuffaLouie’s can’t be beat nor can the pizza at Mother Bears

You can definitely spend the whole time you’re not partying at the wedding eating (and we wouldn’t blame you—there are so many awesome food options and eating is excellent) but if you don’t want to do that (because that would actually be really hard probably) here are some fun things you can do: Visit the Lilly Library, the Lilly Library is the rare book and manuscript library and has the new testament of the Gutenberg Bible, the first printed collection of Shakespeare’s works, the manuscript for Peter Pan, and papers from the likes of Orson Welles, Sylvia Plath, and Edith Wharton • For the young (or just young at heart) visit The Wonderlab • Walk the B-Line Trail for some scenic exercise • Go on a Brewery Tour at Upland Brewing • Get some of that famous Indiana corn: the Bloomington Farmer’s Market is on Saturday from 8am to 1pm and definitely worth a visit
